# UI Aesthetic Concept: National Cheng Kung University

## Core Concept

**"Modern Academia × Warm Heritage"** — a UI that blends precision and clarity (engineering school DNA), warmth and tradition (phoenix flower, Tainan culture), and calm institutional authority. Reference points: Apple Education's restraint, editorial magazine layouts, top-tier global university sites (Stanford, ETH Zürich, NUS). The feeling should be _institutional experience_, not _information portal_.

## Positioning

The design must communicate: top-tier research university, internationally-ready, calm confidence. Avoid the current "noisy admin board" feel — overcrowded layouts, weak hierarchy, red used everywhere until it loses meaning. Replace density with breathing space; replace portal-thinking with student-journey-thinking.

## Color System

Anchor on NCKU's official brand colors but use them with restraint. **Phoenix Red (#E4002B)** is the primary accent — reserved for CTAs, key highlights, and moments of emphasis only. Overuse kills its power. **Signature Maroon (#A31F34)** handles headers, hover states, and secondary emphasis. **Dark Grey (#312D33)** and **Warm Grey (#555559)** carry body text and neutral hierarchy.

Expand the palette with **Soft Ivory (#F8F6F4)** as the dominant background — warmer and less harsh than pure white, which softens the academic tone. **White** is reserved for elevated card surfaces. **Muted Gold (#C6A66B)** appears as a prestige accent in small, deliberate doses (dividers, ornamental marks, awards/heritage contexts). The overall ratio should skew heavily neutral, with red as punctuation rather than wallpaper.

## Typography

Pair an elegant serif for headings (Noto Serif or Playfair Display) with a clean modern sans-serif for body (Inter or Noto Sans). Headings should be large and confident with generous tracking; body text should have ample line-height (1.6–1.75) and comfortable measure. Reduce density everywhere — fewer, larger pieces of text rather than walls of small copy. Establish a clear typographic scale with strong contrast between display, heading, and body sizes.

## Layout & Composition

Adopt a card-based modular system with consistent spacing and clear visual grouping. Sections should alternate rhythmically between ivory base, white surface, and softly tinted blocks to create flow rather than a single uninterrupted wall. Use a structured grid with generous gutters and strong vertical rhythm.

Hero sections should feel cinematic: full-width imagery or quiet looping video, minimal overlay text, one strong CTA. The instinct everywhere should be _focus and breathing space_ over _banner plus dense text_.

## Signature Motif: The Phoenix Curve

The unique identity element is a soft, organic curved shape inspired by the phoenix flower and NCKU's phoenix symbolism. These curves appear as subtle background shapes, hero overlays, and section dividers — never geometric, always flowing. Used sparingly, they become the design's signature and prevent it from looking like another generic university template.

## Components

Buttons use 8–12px radius, with primary as solid Phoenix Red, secondary as outlined red, and hover transitioning to deeper maroon. Cards use 16px radius, soft low-opacity shadows, and padding-heavy interiors. Icons are thin-line with consistent stroke weight and slightly rounded terminals. Form fields and inputs follow the same calm, generously-padded language.

## Motion

Subtle and restrained. Fade-in on scroll, gentle 8–12px upward motion on entry, soft 1.03x scale on button hover. No flashy transitions, no playful bounces, no parallax theatrics. Motion should feel like a confident institution, not a startup landing page.

## Information Architecture Tone

Sections and labels should be reorganized around the student/visitor journey rather than internal admin structures. Translate bureaucratic language into human language: "Start your journey," "Academic life," "Resources," "Support," "Important dates" — language that addresses a person, not a database.

## What to Avoid

Heavy red saturation across the whole interface. Dense link clusters and table-heavy sections. Pure white harsh backgrounds. Small dense text blocks. Generic geometric shapes with no cultural specificity. Stock-photo "diverse students smiling at laptop" energy. Anything that reads as "government portal" or "1990s university homepage modernized just enough."

## Aesthetic Keywords

Minimal academic interface, soft editorial layout, subtle gradients, warm neutral backgrounds, refined typography, structured grid, calm prestige, modern university design, light shadows, elegant red accents, organic curves, breathing space, institutional confidence.
